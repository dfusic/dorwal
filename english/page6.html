The procedure of reproducing a drawing as a unique examplar

The procedure of reproducing a drawing as a unique examplar is

show on the DVD in detail. In case you don't have the

opportunity to print the copy from the DVD in good quality,

you'd better go to a print shop or a good (digital) copy shop

where they will print a copy of the drawing on a high-quality

paper without problems (for the pastel to stick better, the paper

should not be smooth). Photo shops often do not have that kind of

paper you need, so it is desirable that you purchase it yourself.

In order for the reproduction to be good quality, as well as for the

drawing to remain clear in the minutest details, it is necessary to

color all the remaining parts (as shown on the DVD) with the wooden

pastels

Why wooden pastels?

Very small parts of the drawing you can only paint with a well-

sharpened tip of a wooden pastel (wax-color). After you colored

the drawing in good quality and according to you wishes with

pastels, remove slightly the rest of the pastel from the

drawing (this is also shown on the DVD)

After having removed the rest of the pastel, you can start coloring

the drawing with dry pastel. In order to make the coloring simple

and easy, and to achieve a good quality of the transitional shade

of the color, use a make-up remover as well, especially if you

work on smaller drawing. If the drawing is bigger use a small

sponge, because the sponge absorbs the dry pastel in its structure

better than a pad. Always start with the brightest color in order to

achieve the proper mixture of pastels, because you cannot make

dark color bright as easily asyou can make bright color dark.
