Apply dry pastels on a pad or a small sponge, by rubbing it in and

then applying it on the paper with a semi-circular, circular or

some other movement. Hereby you don't have to pay attention to

already colored parts of the drawing because dry pastels don't get

attached to pastels so that the latter remains "clean".

After you finish coloring the pciture with dry pastels, that is:

when you color the whole drawing, fix it with fixative for pastels

or hairspray sprinkling the drawing from distance of 20 - 40cm.

In the beginning sprinkle the main parts, that is: what is already

colored with pastels, from a closer distance (10 - 15cm).

Wait until the fixative is dry.

Already after you sprinkled the drawing with fixative for the first

time, you can recognize the smallest details of the drawing has

"lifted itself up" from pastels. It is necessary to sprinkle the

drawing twice or three times in order to make the dry pastel fix

well and to prevent it from smearing. The final coloring of the

reproduction is done with dry wooden pastel or a sharpened

(harder) chalk pastel, in order to finalize the drawing parts more

precisely and to achieve a three-dimensional effect. After having

finished the last phase of coloring with dry pastel, we sprinke the

drawing gently from a further distance with fixative, so that

the last layer gets fixed on the paper. However, the last layer

cannotget fixed to the extent that it gets impossible to remove it

with fingers or something else because it gets applied in a thicker

layer. Therefore it is recommendable to do the last phase right

before you frame the picture.
